Sell as-is

A difficult property does not need a complicated sale.

We buy homes with major repairs, deferred maintenance, vacancy, liens, and time-sensitive circumstances.

Conditions we evaluate

Roof leaks, water or fire damage, mold concerns, outdated kitchens and systems, foundation problems, code issues, hoarding cleanup, storm damage, vandalism, and long-term vacancy. You do not need to fix the property before requesting an offer.

Behind on payments or facing foreclosure?

Time matters. Request the current payoff and sale date from your lender or servicer, and speak with a qualified attorney or HUD-approved housing counselor. If there is enough time and equity, a property sale may be one possible option—but AssetLift cannot stop a foreclosure, change a lender deadline, or give legal advice.

Avoid foreclosure-rescue scams. Do not sign away ownership, make payments to a buyer, or rely on verbal promises that a sale date has changed. Confirm deadlines directly with your lender, court, sheriff, or attorney.

How an as-is offer is calculated

We consider comparable sales, current condition, repair scope, taxes and carrying costs, title risk, resale expenses, and a margin for uncertainty. That usually means a cash offer is below the possible retail value of a renovated home.

What to share with us

The address, photos if available, known repairs, whether utilities are on, occupancy, mortgage or lien information, and any hard deadline. The more complete the information, the more accurately we can respond.
