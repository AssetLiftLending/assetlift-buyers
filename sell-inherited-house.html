Estate property options

Sell an inherited property without renovating it first.

A direct sale may simplify repairs, cleanout, showings, and coordination among heirs.

Start with authority to sell

The person signing must have legal authority on behalf of the estate or trust. The required documents vary by state and circumstances. An estate or probate attorney can explain who may sign and whether court approval is needed.

Can a property be sold during probate?

Often yes, once the appropriate representative has authority and any required approvals are obtained. The title company or closing attorney will also identify mortgages, liens, taxes, judgments, or estate-related requirements.

Out-of-state heirs

Many steps can be handled remotely. We can evaluate the property from photos, a local contact, or an appointment. Closing professionals can explain remote-signing options.

Contents and repairs

Keep important records, family items, valuables, and anything required by the estate. For the remaining contents, ask whether they may stay under the written agreement. We buy homes with outdated interiors, deferred maintenance, and major repair needs.

Tax note: Inherited-property taxes and basis rules are fact-specific. Speak with a qualified tax professional before choosing a sale strategy.
